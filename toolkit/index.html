05 · Usage Rules

Do & Do Not

These rules protect the integrity of the mark wherever it appears. They apply to everything — workbooks, slides, proposals, social, signage, and co-branded materials.

Do

  • Use the primary navy mark on navy backgrounds; the white-background alternate on white
  • Use the transparent variant on color, pattern, or photo backgrounds
  • Maintain minimum clearspace of 1× the height of “BEYOND” on all sides, measured from the mark’s edge
  • Use the horizontal wordmark for slide footers and document headers
  • Scale proportionally — lock aspect ratio at all times
  • Use only the approved copper values — never orange, gold, or tan
  • Use the exact approved source files from the File Usage Map

Do Not

  • Stretch, skew, or rotate the mark
  • Outline, shadow, or add effects to the mark
  • Place the solid-background mark on busy photo or patterned backgrounds — use the transparent variant
  • Recreate the mark or Compass Star from scratch — always start from the approved files
  • Use any font other than Barlow, Barlow Condensed, or Cormorant Garamond
  • Use copper as a background fill
  • Combine the mark with other logos at equal visual weight
  • Display the mark where “BEYOND” is smaller than 8mm in print
Co-Branding — Promoting a BTM Engagement

When BTM appears alongside your identity, the BTM mark must not compete for dominance. Your mark may appear at equal or greater size; place the BTM mark in a secondary location — footer, inside cover, or closing page. On jointly branded deliverables, use the horizontal wordmark rather than the full mark, and never place the BTM mark directly adjacent to your logo at equal scale.
